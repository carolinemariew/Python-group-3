Introduction¶

The European Union has a big focus on sustainability with the European Green Deal that has been presented. The European union thrives to become climate neutral by the year 2050. When focusing on transport, one of the goals is to have doubled in high-speed rail traffic across Europe (European Commission, 2020). Therefore, for this project, the focus is on the modal shift from air travel to international train travel for the past ten years. Additionally, a case study on France its domestic travel numbers is introduced. France has multiple domestic flights and a vast high speed rail network. France is chosen, because it is the only EU nation that has publically published the data on both domestic aviation, as well as domestic high speed train service. Therefore, a thorough analysis can be made on different geographic scales within the EU.

References:

  1. European Commission. (2020). The Transport and Mobility Sector. In European Commission.

Research Objective¶

Our main research question:
What is the trend in the modal shift from air travel to international train travel from the last ten years within the European Union?

Our sub research questions:
What is the trend of air travel in the last ten years within the European Union?
What is the trend of train travel in the last ten years within the European Union?
What is the correlation between air travel and train travel in the last ten years within the European Union?
What is the trend of the modal shift to high speed train within France?

Data Used¶

After collecting a lot of datasets, it was narrowed down to three datasets.

  1. Air travel within Europe https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/databrowser/view/TTR00012/default/line?lang=en
  2. International train travel within Europe https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/databrowser/view/rail_pa_typepas__custom_7854806/default/table?lang=en
  3. High speed train travel France https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/databrowser/view/RAIL_PA_SPEED/default/table?lang=en

The data collected for air travel within Europe accounts for flights from the EU to outside the EU, flights within the EU and domestic flights. In order to make a distinction between the different catagories, the following distrubition has been used:

https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/index.php?title=Air_transport_statistics

Disclaimer:
Not all the datasets are complete. The datasets for international train travel lacks data of the following countries: Hungary, The Netherlands and Belgium. Therefore, the analysis will not represent a complete real world situation.

Data Pipeline¶

All the data needs to be combined in a graph over time with stamps for places when policies have changed. The passenger number difference is the greatest indicator of our main question. When considering the data, it is important to acknowledge that policy is not the only factor influencing passenger numbers, like ticket price and the COVID-19 pandemic.

Steps:

  1. Data collection
  2. Data cleaning and transformation
  3. Data integration into a single dataset
  4. Data aggregation for the most interesting time periods
  5. Data analysis
  6. Data enrichment with external factors, such as COVID events
  7. Data presentation in graphs

Graph explained
When looking at the comparison between inter EU air passengers and railway passengers, a few things stand out. First of all, the increase over the last ten years, air travel shows a significant increase in the amount of passengers. In contrast, the railway passenger level stays almost constant until COVID-19. Moreover, there is a difference in the recovery after COVID-19. When looking at air travel, the amount of passengers is going back to the amount before COVID-19. This is not the case for railway passengers. This is still not even half way back to the level before COVID-19. This raises questions about the goals of the EU concerning the doubling of the amount of highspeed trains by the year 2030.

Graphs explained
When you look at the graphs of air and train passengers per month over time in the EU, a few countries stand out. When focussing on air travel Malta and Cyprus stand out compared to the other EU contries. This makes sense considering that both countries are islands and therefore do not have the option of train travel to other countries. Moreover, the countries with low amounts of air travel passengers are mostly Eastern European countries. In these countries the general population has less capita to travel by plane.
In the graph for train passengers, Luxembourg has by far the highest numbers. An explanation for this can be the fact that Luxembourg provides free plublic transport for everyone, including foreigners. Complementary, almost 47% of the workforce in Luxembourg live in neighbouring countries (Luxembourg Times, 2023), which supports the urge to international train travel. Another noteworthy trend is seen in Austria. The amount of train passengers in Austria is more after COVID-19 than before COVID-19. This is the opposite from most countries, where the amount of passengers is still not back on the same previously achieved level after COVID-19.

Part 4 - Conclusion¶

In this final part, the sub research questions will be discussed and the main research question will be answered.

Our sub research questions:
What is the trend of air travel in the last ten years within the European Union?
When looking at the trend of air travel in parts 1 and 2, air travel increased consistently before COVID-19. However, air travel did recover fast after COVID-19 but is still not at the same level of before COVID-19. Therefore, there is no visible trend that travelers swifted away from air travel to other transport means.

What is the trend of train travel in the last ten years within the European Union?
When looking at the trend of train travel in parts 1 and 2, train travel before COVID-19 kept almost consitent over the years. In some countries however, it even became evident that there was a decline. When COVID-19 hit, the decrease in train travel was significant and has not recoverd as of the end of 2022.

What is the correlation between air travel and train travel in the last ten years within the European Union?
There is a big different in countries when looking at the correlation. When looking at the regression graph in part 2, southern Europe is doing a lot worse than central Europe and Northern Europe. An explanation for this can be the attraction of tourists, mainly traveling by train over larger distances, to the southern European countries. When looking at the population of the countries, France is doing the best out of the bigger countries. Notably, Slovakia is the only country with more international trains than inter EU flights.

What is the trend of the modal shift to high speed train in France?
When looking at the domestic trend in France, there are more passengers travelling via rail than via air. For railway passengers the data for 2022 is missing. Therefore it is hard to conclude how domestic train travel has recovered after COVID-19.

Our main research question:
What is the trend in the modal shift from air travel to international train travel from the last ten years within the European Union?
A shift from international train to air travel is hardly visible, which suspects that this situation does not comply with the goals set by the European Union. COVID-19 had a large influence on both air and rail, although air is recovering faster. Thus, the total European image shows a rather pessimistic trend for international rail advocates. Still, for smaller domestic distances, France proves to be a optimistic example on how trains can form a competetive alternative to planes. Moreover, France scores well on international train passenger numbers as well, which suspects that a robust and competetive domestic network could boost international travel numbers, too.
